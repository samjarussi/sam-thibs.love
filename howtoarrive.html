Arriving in Paris by plane

  • Arriving in CDG (Charles de Gaulle) and getting to Montparnasse

    If you are flying into CDG airport, you can take the RER Line B to Denfert-Rochereau, and then change for the Metro line 6 (Direction Charles de Gaulle-Étoile) until station Montparnasse-Bienvenüe.
    For more information, please visit this website.

  • Arriving in Orly and getting to Montparnasse

    The easiest way is to take the OrlyBus to Denfert-Rochereau and then switch to Metro line 6 (Direction Charles de Gaulle-Étoile) to Montparnasse-Bienvenüe station.
    More information here.

By train

    First things first...

  • Gare de Lyon to Montparnasse

    Take Line 1 metro towards La Défense, get off at Châtelet, switch trains, and take Line 4 towards Bagneaux to Montparnasse-Bienvenüe station. This trip should take about 25-30 minutes.

  • Gare du Nord to Montparnasse

    Take Line 4 metro towards Bagneaux directly to Montparnasse-Bienvenue station. This trip should take about 25 minutes.

  • Montparnasse to Concarneau

    If you're traveling from Montparnasse to Concarneau, taking a train to Rosporden is the best option, with a travel time of around 4 hours. Once you arrive in Rosporden, let us know and we'll come to your rescue.
    You can purchase your train tickets on this website.
